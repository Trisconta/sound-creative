# Get 'Em Out by Friday - Bullet-Point Cheat Sheet

## Main Characters
- John Pebble
  - Head of Styx Enterprises
  - Symbol of corporate greed
- Mark Hall (The Winkler)
  - Pebble's agent
  - Executes evictions and corporate orders
- Mrs. Barrow
  - Tenant being forced out
  - Represents ordinary people harmed by housing policies
- Genetic Control
  - Future authority manipulating human biology
  - Represents dystopian bureaucracy and dehumanization

---

## 1. The Eviction
- The Winkler tells Mrs. Barrow she must leave her home
- Goal: raise rents and replace tenants with more profitable ones
- Tone: cold and bureaucratic
- Sets up the critique of predatory landlords

---

## 2. Corporate Scheming
- John Pebble congratulates The Winkler
- Their shared goal: maximize profit at any human cost
- Satire of corporate success built on exploitation
- Highlights moral emptiness of the system

---

## 3. Jump to the Future (2012)
- Introduction of Genetic Control
- Plan: shrink humans to 4 feet tall
- Purpose:
  - Fit more people into the same buildings
  - Increase density
  - Charge more rent
- Dark humor: humans treated as adjustable commodities

---

## 4. Final Commentary
- Narrator reflects with bitter irony
- The powerful keep profiting
- The vulnerable keep suffering
- Message: the system changes methods but not behavior

---

## Themes
- Corporate greed
- Housing injustice
- Dehumanization
- Bureaucratic satire
- Theatrical storytelling typical of early Genesis
